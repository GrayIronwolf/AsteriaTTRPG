# Iron Ingot

## Overview
Iron Ingot is a common processed metal material used throughout Asteria for basic smithing, structural fittings, weapons, armor, tools, and settlement repairs.

## Appearance
An Iron Ingot is a dark silver-grey bar with a dull metallic finish, forge scale along its edges, and occasional blackened hammer marks or casting seams.

## Source & Processing
Iron Ingots are usually made by smelting Iron Ore in a forge or bloomery, removing impurities, and casting the workable metal into standardized bars.

## Crafting Grade
Iron Ingot is a basic grade material. It is reliable, widely available, and easy for most trained smiths to shape, but it lacks the superior edge retention or arcane receptivity of rarer metals.

## Uses
Iron Ingots are used for practical construction, reinforcement, common tools, chains, nails, hinges, buckles, cookware, and the internal fittings of many crafted objects.

## Alchemy Uses
Powdered or shaved iron may be used in grounding compounds, warding circles, magnetic experiments, and low-grade Earth-aspected preparations.

## Crafting Uses
Iron Ingots are core inputs for basic weapons, basic armor, shields, traps, tool heads, wagon fittings, door hardware, and forge repairs.

## Culinary Uses
Iron Ingot has no culinary use.

## Magical Affinities
Iron carries a faint Earth affinity. It is stable, grounding, and resistant to volatile magical resonance, making it useful in mundane equipment and simple warded fixtures.

## Market Value
Iron Ingots are inexpensive near mines and smithing towns, but their value rises in remote settlements, war camps, and regions with poor access to metal deposits.
