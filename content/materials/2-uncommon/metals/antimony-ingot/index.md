# Antimony Ingot

## Overview
Antimony Ingot is an uncommon processed material used for specialised alloy work, alchemical vessels, and crafting projects that need hard but brittle reinforcement.

## Appearance
An Antimony Ingot appears as a dark silver-purple bar with a glassy metallic sheen, sharp edge highlights, and fine fractured patterns across its surface.

## Source & Processing
Antimony Ingots are made by smelting Antimony Ore under controlled conditions, then casting the refined material into standard bars for trade or workshop use.

## Crafting Grade
Antimony Ingot is a specialised grade material. It is useful for hardening and stabilising, but careless use can make finished work brittle.

## Uses
Antimony Ingots are used in alloy hardening, reinforced tool edges, ritual fixtures, alchemical vessels, and experimental smithing.

## Alchemy Uses
Shavings or powder from Antimony Ingots can be used in binding salts, purging reagents, and stabilising compounds.

## Crafting Uses
Antimony Ingots are best used as a secondary metal in alloys rather than as a primary weapon or armor base.

## Culinary Uses
Antimony Ingot has no culinary use and should not be handled as food-safe material.

## Magical Affinities
Antimony holds faint Earth and Shadow affinities, making it useful where a darker or stabilising mineral resonance is required.

## Market Value
Antimony Ingots are moderately valuable and usually traded through smithing guilds, alchemists, and mining settlements with specialist furnaces.
