# Antimony Ore

## Overview
Antimony Ore is an uncommon mineral resource used by smiths and alchemists for hardening alloys, stabilising volatile mixtures, and producing specialised metalwork.

## Appearance
Antimony Ore forms dark metallic crystal clusters with purple-blue highlights and sharp fractured faces. Rich samples can look almost glassy under lamplight.

## Deposit & Habitat
Antimony Ore is usually found in narrow mineral veins, deep mine pockets, old volcanic seams, and shadow-touched stone layers.

## Mining
Miners extract Antimony Ore carefully because brittle crystals can fracture during rough handling. Good samples are sorted away from waste stone before smelting.

## Refinement
Antimony Ore is smelted into Antimony Ingots or blended into harder alloy batches. Poor ventilation can make processing hazardous.

## Uses
Antimony Ore is valued for alloy work, alchemical powders, stabilising compounds, and specialised crafting where brittle hardness is useful.

## Crafting Uses
Antimony Ore refines into Antimony Ingots and can be used in alloy hardening, tool reinforcement, and controlled experimental smithing.

## Alchemy Uses
Powdered Antimony Ore may be used in purging reagents, binding salts, and stabilising preparations that need a sharp mineral anchor.

## Magical Affinities
Antimony carries faint Earth and Shadow affinities. It is usually quiet but can hold darker resonance better than common iron.

## Market Value
Antimony Ore has moderate value and is most available near specialised mining settlements or alchemical trade routes.
